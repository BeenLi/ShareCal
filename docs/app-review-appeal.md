## Reason for Appeal

```text
Dear App Review Board,

I am appealing the rejection of ShareCal, iOS version 1.0 (3), submission ID bfe2e590-d9a5-41eb-9bc8-12c99f9411b6, under Guideline 4.3(a).

ShareCal is not a duplicate, repackaged, or template-based app. It is an original SwiftUI app built for private two-person calendar coordination. After researching the iOS calendar options available to users in China, we found that common native calendar solutions, including Apple Calendar, do not satisfy our target workflow: they do not provide a dedicated side-by-side schedule view for two invited users, and they do not support comments on shared events. ShareCal addresses that specific gap by using EventKit and CloudKit sharing so two invited users can privately mirror only the calendars they choose, view both schedules side by side, send event invitations, and comment on shared events. Calendar sharing is controlled by the user, and shared data stays in the user's local device storage and Apple iCloud/CloudKit share. The app does not use advertising SDKs, third-party analytics, tracking SDKs, or a repackaged third-party calendar template.

We believe the original review may have misunderstood the app as a generic calendar or template app because the first submission did not clearly explain the private iCloud sharing workflow and couple-focused collaboration features. The app's purpose is a distinct private coordination workflow for two invited users, not another general public calendar clone or a set of multiple similar apps.

We respectfully request that the App Review Board re-evaluate this submission under Guideline 4.3(a). We are also ready to provide additional review steps, screenshots, or an updated build with clearer onboarding and reviewer sample data if that helps demonstrate the unique functionality.

Thank you.
```

## Apple Guidance Used

- Apple says appeals are appropriate when the developer believes App Review misunderstood the app's concept or functionality, or the review was unfair.
- Apple asks developers to provide specific reasons why the app complies with the App Review Guidelines.
- Apple asks developers to respond to requests for additional information before submitting an appeal.
